Stroll For Your Life

The ultimate goal of this project was to create something that combined a number of unique modern technologies while also creating an intricate asynchronous coop experience. The final product ended up becoming a proof of concept and an excellent research opportunity. It allows for opportunities to look at how people interact in VR spaces when given a limited set of tools and senses. It's the project I am most proud of in my portfolio and therefore it deserves a thorough explanation!

The game puts one player in a VR headset and a second player by the Microsoft Surface SUR40. From now on these will be referred to as the 'Immersed" and "Illuminator" respectively as to not confuse the two. Below you can see our experimental setup in our development studio.


Future Work

This project was so extremley well recieved not only by the visualization staff and professors at our University, but also the people testing it so we decided to unanimously keep working on it beyond the course scope during Spring of 2018. This project will be submitted to a number of game design/visualization/interaction competitions as well as become a benchmark within the Visualization Studio at KTH of what is possible when using the technology available. The plan is to add 3D printed models that can be recognized on the board and to test monitoring the player walking using the Microsoft Kinect and have the speed in which they move be reflected within the game in real time. There will also be two research projects carried out in regards to the project. We expect to create scientific publicactions and submit these to an appropriate Journal for peer checking.
